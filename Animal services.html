Lost and Found Pets

Lost Pets

If your are a pet owner trying to find your lost pet there are several things you can do:
1. Give us a call at (123)456-7890 with a description of your pet so we can try to locate it
2. Visit our animal shelter at 123 San Jose State during operational hours to see if we have your furry friend
3. Visit our adoption page on our website to see if your dog is in our database
4. Fill out a Lost and Found Pet Form online and we'll try our best to locate your animal

Tips to Finding Lost Pets:
- Dont panic! Many pets go months before being reunited with their owner
- You can print out lost animal fliers and post it where the pet was last seen

Found Pets

Happy Tails is currently accepting sick or injured pets. If a lost pet is found there are several things you can do to help get the lost pet back home:
1. Lost and Found Pet Form online and send us a picture so we can try to find their owners.
2. Bring the lost pet in to our shelter during operational hours.
3. If you cannot move the animal or think that doing so could put you in danger, contact us at (123)456-7890 so that we can come and take the animal back home.

Other things to note:
- To increase the chances of reuniting the lost pet with its owner, we will take several measures including photographing and recording all stray animals, adding their details to our database, showcasing them on our website, and checking for a microchip.
- After taking the stray pet you found to the shelter for processing, you have the option to take care of the animal in your own home. Additionally, posting flyers in your neighborhood or the location where you found the pet could also be beneficial.
- If you found an injured stray animal after our operational hours, please take it to a 24/7 vet clinic.

Surrendering Pets

If you are considering surrender, we ask that you exhaust all your options before starting the surrender process.

Surrendering Process:

Step 1: Contact us to discuss Surrendering

Owners need to fill out an online Pet Owner Surrender Application. Once we've recieved your application, we will reach out to discuss your pet's behavior and health history and ask questions regarding your pet. Please be prepared to send medical records. We will also schedule an in-person evaluation with your dog to further assess his behavior.

Step 2: Surrendering Your Pet

After reviewing all of the information and in-person evaluation. We will contact you on whether or not your pet has been approved to enter our adoption program. If approved, we will contact you to schedule an appointment for surrender. Please be prepared to pay a surrender fee at the time of the intake to help care for your pet. Surrender fees - Dog or Cat: $100- $230 - Rodent: $20 Surrendering your pet can be stressful for both you and your animal. We encourage that owners bring your pets favorite unwashed toy blanket or bed.

Step 3: In the shelter

After recieving your pet, all animals will recieve a health and behavior assessment to determine their needs in the shelter and in a new home.

Step 4: Adoption

If applicable, your pet will be placed for adoption. When a potential adopter is interested in any of our pets we'll do a thorough evaluation and spend our time getting to know them before allowing them to take our animals home.
